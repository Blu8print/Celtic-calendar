About the Calendar

Time, the way nature intended it.

The Gregorian calendar is a political artifact. December means "tenth month" — it is the twelfth. October means "eighth" — it is the tenth. The original Roman calendar had ten months starting in March. Two more were inserted, the names were never updated, and nobody has questioned it in over two thousand years. The months are unequal, the weeks drift, and the whole system exists because a committee of emperors and popes kept patching it.

The Celtic Tree Calendar is different. Thirteen months. Twenty-eight days each. Every month is exactly four weeks, every week begins on the same day of the year, and each month is named for a sacred tree carrying its own teaching.

Roots Calendar brings this ancient system to your pocket — as a fully functional calendar alongside your everyday life.

Features

Ancient rhythm, modern functionality.

🌳
13 Sacred Tree Months
Beth (Birch) through Ruis (Elder) — each month carries its own tree, Ogham character, and teaching. Navigate a full year of natural wisdom.
📅
Month, Week & Day Views
Full calendar grid, 3-day view, week view, day time-grid, and schedule view. Your events in all the views you're used to.
🔄
Google Calendar Sync
Your existing events appear in Celtic time. Create new events that sync back to Google Calendar — all from your device, no intermediary servers.
🔥
Celtic Festivals
Imbolc, Beltane, Lughnasadh, Samhain, and the solar festivals — all built in as read-only system events, correctly mapped to their Celtic positions.
🌙
Moon Phase Indicators
Pure mathematical moon phase calculation — no external API. See the current phase in every day cell and header, accurate to the hour.
🔒
Private by Design
All event data stays on your device in local SQLite storage. Google sync is optional. No account required, no cloud backend, no tracking.

The Story

Built by a maker, for people who feel time differently.

Roots Calendar started when I heard a podcast about the number 13 and why it got erased from our calendar. That sent me down a rabbit hole into Celtic culture, the Beth-Luis-Nion tree calendar, and eventually — as these things go — into a Flutter codebase at midnight.

The app is open-source, built in Flutter, and published under the GPL-3.0 license. It's a side project I'm genuinely proud of: something that blends a real historical system with careful, modern software craft.

Right now it's in closed beta on Android. iOS is on the roadmap. If you want to help test it and you have a Gmail address, that's all you need.
